How Fixed vs. Floating Arbitrage Works in DeFi

In traditional markets, traders earn carry by receiving a fixed rate and paying a floating rate. DeFi lets you do the same, entirely on-chain, using Pendle to lock a fixed yield and Aave to finance it with a floating-rate loan. Below I explain the intuition, show the core math, and walk through an example and the risks.

Estimated read time · 8–10 min

The intuition

You buy a Principal Token (PT) on Pendle that redeems for 1 unit of the underlying stable at maturity, and because it trades at a discount today, it embeds a fixed yield. You fund this purchase by borrowing the same stablecoin on Aave at a floating rate. the idea is that if you hold the PT to maturity, then your P&L is the fixed yield you locked minus the floating rate you paid (net of fees). Conceptually, you’ve built an on-chain receive-fixed / pay-float swap that is delta-neutral in stables.

How Pendle PTs deliver a fixed rate

Pendle splits a yield-bearing asset into a Principal Token (PT) and a Yield Token (YT). The PT pays back exactly 1 unit at maturity T and trades at a discount bearing an interest rate. If today’s PT price is P_PT < 1, the effective annual fixed rate is:

r_fix = P_PT^{-1/T} − 1
For short maturities, a handy approximation is r_fix ≈ (1 − P_PT) / (P_PT · T).

Economically, buying a PT is like buying a zero-coupon bond that repays 1 at maturity. The discount you lock in today is your fixed return.

Financing with a floating-rate loan

On Aave, borrow rates float with utilization. If your average realized borrow over the life of the trade is r_var, and your notional is N, the fixed–floating carry over the term is:

P&L(0→T) ≈ N · (r_fix − r_var) · T − c · N
c aggregates fees, slippage, funding spreads—quoted as a % of notional.

A useful monitoring metric is the floating-rate breakeven:

r_var* = r_fix − c / T
If the live borrow goes above r_var*, your carry turns negative—time to reduce, refinance, or close.

Execution flow (fixed vs. floating)

  1. Borrow the stablecoin (e.g., USDe) on Aave at floating APY r_var.
  2. Swap that stable into PT–USDe (same underlying) on Pendle at price P_PT.
  3. Hold the PT to maturity → redeem 1 USDe per PT → repay Aave principal + interest.

Structurally, you’re long “receive-fixed” (the PT) and short “pay-float” (the loan). There’s no directional bet on crypto prices or rates— just a spread between independently set fixed and floating markets.

Worked example (illustrative)

Suppose PT–USDe (maturity in ≈33 days) implies a fixed yield of r_fix = 15.49%, while Aave’s USDe borrow sits near r_var = 7.67%. On N = $1,000,000, with all-in fees c = 0.20% and T = 33/365 years: 

P&L ≈ 1,000,000 × (0.1549 − 0.0767) × (33/365) − 0.002 × 1,000,000
≈ 7,070 − 2,000 = $5,070  (for 33 days)
Annualized ≈ 5.61% net
Breakeven r_var* = 0.1549 − (0.002 / (33/365)) ≈ 13.28%

The position earns positive carry so long as your realized floating rate stays below ~13.3% and you hold the PT to maturity.

What can go wrong (and how to mitigate it)

  • Rate risk: the floating borrow spikes and compresses your spread.
    Mitigation: breakeven guard (r_var*), rate alerts, option to refinance to a cheaper venue.
  • Early-exit risk: the PT’s fixed payoff is only guaranteed at maturity; selling early can realize a loss if PT prices move against you.
    Mitigation: size for hold-to-maturity; avoid forced unwinds; plan liquidity buffers.
  • Asset/issuer risk: the stablecoin (e.g., USDe) could depeg.
    Mitigation: depeg monitoring, diversified issuers, max exposure limits.
  • Liquidity & slippage: thin books on the PT series you need.
    Mitigation: stage entries, set max slippage, trade during deeper liquidity windows.
  • Smart-contract risk: protocol bugs or governance issues.
    Mitigation: prioritize audited protocols, track bounty programs, cap per-protocol risk.

Why the opportunity exists

Pendle’s fixed rates are set in a market for discounted PTs. Aave’s floating rates are set by money-market utilization. Because these mechanisms are independent, the same underlying stablecoin can have a rich fixed rate on Pendle while the floating borrow on Aave remains cheap. The arbitrage is simply the disciplined harvesting of that structural dispersion.

Math appendix (derivations)

PT fixed rate: PT redeems 1 at maturity T. If today’s price is P_PT:

r_fix = (1 / P_PT)^{1/T} − 1

First-order: r_fix ≈ (1 − P_PT) / (P_PT · T) for small T.

Carry P&L over the term:

P&L(0→T) = N · (r_fix − r_var) · T − c · N

Floating breakeven: set P&L = 0 ⇒ r_var* = r_fix − c/T.

Final thought

Fixed–floating arbitrage in DeFi isn’t about heroics; it’s about process: lock a real fixed, fund it cheaply, monitor the breakeven, and respect liquidity and issuer risk. As the ecosystem matures, these “boring” spreads are likely to become the backbone of professional on-chain yield strategies.
