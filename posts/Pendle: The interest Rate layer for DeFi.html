Pendle: The Interest Rate Layer for DeFi

How Pendle turns passive DeFi yield into a tradable financial instrument, and what that means for on-chain fixed income.

Estimated read time · 15 min

Introduction

Decentralized finance has introduced a wide range of yield-bearing assets, including liquid staking tokens, restaking assets, and synthetic dollar instruments. While these assets generate yield, that yield is typically variable and realized passively over time, limiting users' ability to actively manage interest rate exposure or access future returns immediately.

In traditional finance, interest rates and yield streams are actively traded through fixed-income instruments such as bonds, interest rate swaps, and forwards. These markets allow participants to lock in fixed returns, hedge interest rate risk, or speculate on future rate movements. Until recently, equivalent functionality did not exist in DeFi, where yield remained largely non-transferable and dependent on passive holding. In other words, yield in DeFi was highly illiquid, limiting the range of possible financial operations and the overall growth of the ecosystem.

Pendle addresses this limitation by enabling the tokenization and trading of future yield, transforming yield from a passive return into a tradable financial instrument. This enables users to lock in fixed yields, speculate on future yield changes, or access anticipated returns upfront to improve capital efficiency. Pendle introduces interest rate markets into DeFi, allowing yield to be priced, traded, and managed dynamically through market mechanisms.

How It Works: PT and YT

Pendle operates by splitting a yield-bearing asset into two separate tokens:

  • Principal Token (PT): Represents ownership of the underlying asset and can be redeemed for its full value at a predefined maturity date.
  • Yield Token (YT): Represents the right to receive all yield generated by the underlying asset until maturity.

These tokens can then be traded independently within Pendle's marketplace. A concrete example illustrates the mechanics:

Component Description Approximate value
Deposit $1,000 sETH (5% APY). Market yield on Pendle: 6% $1,000
Principal Token (PT) Redeemable for $1,000 at maturity, trading at a discount today ~$940
Yield Token (YT) Right to receive all yield generated until maturity ~$60

This structure enables a range of financial strategies. Users seeking predictable returns may sell their YT and retain PT, effectively converting variable yield into a fixed return. Conversely, users expecting higher future yields may purchase YT to gain leveraged exposure to yield fluctuations. This separation allows market participants to manage yield exposure according to their individual risk preferences, market expectations, or financial needs.

The Pendle AMM

Pendle facilitates these trades through a custom automated market maker specifically designed for yield-bearing assets. Unlike traditional AMMs, Pendle's AMM incorporates time to maturity into its pricing mechanism, allowing token prices to converge toward redemption value as maturity approaches. This enables efficient price discovery for future yield and ensures consistent liquidity throughout the asset's lifecycle.

Adoption and Ecosystem Growth

Pendle's growth has closely tracked the expansion of yield-bearing assets across DeFi, particularly with the rise of liquid staking, restaking, and synthetic yield protocols. Since launching V2, Pendle has grown into the largest on-chain yield trading platform by total value locked. The protocol currently operates across ten blockchains — Ethereum, Arbitrum, BSC, Base, Sonic, Berachain, HyperEVM, Plasma, Mantle, and Optimism — and is actively expanding to non-EVM ecosystems including Solana via cross-chain principal tokens. With 552 active markets spanning assets from Lido's stETH to Ethena's sUSDe, Pendle has established itself as core infrastructure for on-chain interest rate exposure.

Boros: Expansion into Funding Rate Markets

Beyond its core yield tokenization product, Pendle launched Boros — a funding-rate derivatives platform designed to bring institutional-grade rate markets to decentralized finance. Boros tokenizes perpetual funding rates, transforming an untradable yield stream into a tradable instrument. This targets the $150B+/day perpetual swap market, a significantly larger addressable market than spot yield alone. By year-end 2025, Boros had achieved approximately $6.9 billion in open interest, $91 million in deposits, and generated $6.8 million in fees — demonstrating early product-market fit for rate derivatives on-chain.

Institutional Adoption

Pendle introduced KYC-compliant Citadels in 2025, offering regulated access to on-chain fixed income through partnerships with investment managers. The protocol is also exploring Shariah-compliant offerings targeting the $4.5 trillion Islamic finance market. These moves position Pendle to capture institutional capital that requires compliance infrastructure before entering DeFi.

Tokenomics and Incentive Structure

PENDLE's native token plays a central role in coordinating incentives and governance, designed to align token holders with the growth and economic performance of the platform.

Revenue Model

Pendle generates revenue primarily through transaction fees charged on trades executed within its AMM. Each swap involving PT or YT incurs a fee, paid by users trading yield exposure, contributing directly to protocol revenue. Because Pendle functions as a marketplace for yield, its revenue is closely tied to trading activity across its pools. Periods of elevated demand — driven by increased DeFi participation or shifting yield expectations — typically result in higher trading volumes and greater fee generation. Conversely, when market activity slows and fewer participants actively manage yield exposure, trading volumes and protocol revenue tend to decline.

Staking Mechanism

PENDLE accrues value through its staking mechanism. Users can stake PENDLE to receive sPENDLE, a token representing their staked position. Stakers become eligible to receive a share of protocol-generated fees and additional incentives distributed across Pendle's liquidity pools. This structure directly links tokenholder rewards to the level of activity occurring within the protocol. As trading volume and fee generation increase, the rewards distributed to stakers grow correspondingly, strengthening the incentive to hold and stake the token.

Reflexive Dynamics

Higher protocol activity generates higher fees, which increases staking rewards, strengthens demand for the token, and reduces circulating supply as more users choose to stake. This pushes token value upward. Conversely, when activity declines, fee generation falls, reducing the economic incentive to hold and stake PENDLE. Notably, PENDLE's full token supply is already unlocked, eliminating future dilution risk and linking token value more directly to protocol usage and fee generation.

The relationship between PENDLE's price and broader DeFi conditions is visible when overlaying PENDLE against total DeFi TVL. Through mid-2025, both rose in tandem; however, from late 2025 onward, PENDLE's price declined more sharply than overall DeFi TVL, suggesting token-specific headwinds — including the vePENDLE to sPENDLE migration uncertainty and broader DeFi yield compression — beyond general market conditions.

Transition to sPENDLE

Historically, Pendle used a vote-escrowed token model (vePENDLE), but in January 2026 it transitioned to a liquid staking model through sPENDLE. This structure allows users to stake PENDLE without fixed lock-up periods while still receiving rewards and participating in governance. The change improves capital efficiency and lowers barriers to participation, expanding the pool of long-term tokenholders and strengthening the connection between protocol growth and token demand. Holders of sPENDLE can also deploy those tokens elsewhere in DeFi, adding further utility.

Growth in staked supply reflects increasing participation. Cumulative staked PENDLE grew from approximately 60–70 million tokens in early 2025 to over 120 million by February 2026, representing roughly half of PENDLE's circulating supply. This level of staking participation provides a strong supply-side constraint that amplifies the reflexive dynamics described above.

Performance Relative to Major Cryptoassets

PENDLE has materially outperformed DeFi peers mid-cycle but underperformed late-2025, reflecting its unique sensitivity to yield volatility rather than TVL alone. This distinction matters: unlike protocols whose revenue scales with TVL, Pendle's revenue scales with yield volatility and trading activity. When yields move in either direction, Pendle benefits from the resulting trading activity. When yields are flat and predictable, there is less incentive to trade, and Pendle's value proposition weakens relative to peers.

PENDLE has exhibited greater volatility than BTC, ETH, and SOL, reflecting its direct exposure to DeFi yield conditions rather than broader macro flows. While Bitcoin and Ethereum often reflect institutional capital flows into crypto, PENDLE's performance is more closely tied to conditions within yield-generating sectors of DeFi.

Conclusion

Pendle introduces transferable yield to decentralized finance, enabling users to separate, trade, and manage future yield in a way previously limited to traditional fixed-income markets. By allowing market participants to lock in fixed returns or speculate on yield movements, Pendle expands the range of financial strategies available on-chain and establishes foundational infrastructure for interest rate markets in DeFi.

The 2025 results — $58 billion in fixed yield settled, $5.7 billion average TVL, and $40 million in annualized revenue — demonstrate significant product-market fit. The expansion into funding rate derivatives via Boros, non-EVM chains, and institutional products signals that Pendle is positioning itself not merely as a DeFi application, but as foundational infrastructure for on-chain fixed income.

As yield markets grow in depth and sophistication, the central question is whether Pendle can sustain trading activity through yield compression cycles, or whether its value proposition depends on persistent yield volatility. The answer will determine whether Pendle becomes a cycle-specific application or a permanent layer of DeFi's financial stack.
