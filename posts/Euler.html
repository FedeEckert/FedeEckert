Euler: Modular Credit Infrastructure for DeFi

Euler V2 turns lending into a modular, permissionless system of isolated credit vaults. Below I explain the motivation, how it works (EVK + EVC), how interest rates are modeled, why it differs from Morpho, and why it matters—backed by live usage metrics.

Published: 31 Oct 2025 · 10–12 min

Motivation — the problem Euler set out to solve

Traditional pooled lenders concentrate many assets into one risk surface. That creates three issues: (1) contagion—a failure in one asset can threaten the whole pool; (2) listing rigidity—new or long-tail assets struggle to get markets; and (3) one-size-fits-all rate curves that misprice risk. Euler’s answer in V2 is a toolkit to launch isolated, customizable vaults for virtually any ERC-20, along with infrastructure to connect them safely. [oai_citation:0‡docs.euler.finance](https://docs.euler.finance/introduction/?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

How Euler works — EVK vaults and the EVC “credit graph”

EVK: Euler Vault Kit (vaults as isolated lending pools)

The Euler Vault Kit (EVK) lets anyone deploy a lending vault that follows ERC-4626 share accounting but adds borrowing. Conceptually, there are two kinds of vaults: a liability vault (where lenders deposit the asset that can be borrowed) and collateral vaults (where users park assets that can be recognized as collateral by specific liability vaults). Crucially, each liability vault picks which collateral vaults it accepts—so risk is isolated per vault. [oai_citation:1‡docs.euler.finance](https://docs.euler.finance/developers/evk/?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

EVC: Ethereum Vault Connector (orchestrating multi-vault positions)

The EVC is the router/entry point that stitches vaults together: it enables batching of actions, sub-accounts, and cross-vault checks, so a user can (in one call) deposit collateral in vault X and borrow from vault Y with health checks at the end. This is what turns a set of isolated vaults into a flexible credit graph. [oai_citation:2‡docs.euler.finance](https://docs.euler.finance/concepts/core/evc/?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

Security note: both EVK and EVC have undergone third-party audits (OpenZeppelin, ChainSecurity), and Euler runs a large bug bounty program for core components. [oai_citation:3‡openzeppelin.com](https://www.openzeppelin.com/news/euler-vault-kit-evk-audit?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

Interest-rate models — how pricing adapts to utilisation

Euler V2 supports multiple Interest Rate Models (IRM) at the vault level. A common design is a linear-kink curve (gradual increase until a utilisation threshold, then a steeper slope), but other reactive models are available. The key idea is that each vault can select a curve appropriate to its asset/liquidity profile, rather than sharing one global model. [oai_citation:4‡docs.euler.finance](https://docs.euler.finance/concepts/financial/interest-rates?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

The intuition is standard: let U = borrowed / supplied. As U rises, borrow rates increase to attract supply and temper demand; as U falls, rates ease. Where allowed, a smooth model of the form rb(U) = a + b·Uⁿ can be configured to avoid hard shocks; for stable pairs one might use a gentler slope (n≈1), while volatile assets can use steeper curves. (Euler’s docs enumerate the built-in IRMs and the linear-kink baseline.) [oai_citation:5‡docs.euler.finance](https://docs.euler.finance/concepts/financial/interest-rates?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

User flows — lender and borrower, step by step

Lender (liability vault)

  1. Deposit the asset into a liability vault → receive ERC-4626 shares.
  2. Accrue yield as borrowers draw and pay interest; assets-per-share increases.
  3. Withdraw when liquidity allows (utilisation affects exit smoothness).

Many integrators route through EVC for batching and portfolio operations (e.g., deposit in vault A, simulate, then move). [oai_citation:6‡docs.euler.finance](https://docs.euler.finance/developers/evk/interacting-with-vaults?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

Borrower (via EVC)

  1. Post collateral into one or more collateral vaults.
  2. Borrow from a liability vault that whitelists those collateral vaults.
  3. Maintain health; liquidations are handled per-vault (oracle, LTV, penalty) if the position falls below thresholds.

Because each liability vault decides its accepted collateral map, risk is constrained by design. [oai_citation:7‡docs.euler.finance](https://docs.euler.finance/developers/evk/?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

How Euler differs from Morpho

  • Market creation vs. curation: Euler’s EVK/EVC enables permissionless vault creation and arbitrary credit graphs; Morpho’s current stack emphasizes curated market lists and allocator-managed vaults (MetaMorpho) on top of its isolated Blue markets.
  • Where the risk box lives: Euler isolates risk at the vault and allows each liability vault to define its accepted collateral vaults; Morpho isolates at the market (pair) and then aggregates via vaults that whitelist markets and allocate across them.
  • Rate modeling emphasis: Euler surfaces multiple IRMs per vault (including linear-kink and reactive); Morpho historically focused on P2P optimisation (Optimizer) and now on vault-level allocation across markets. [oai_citation:8‡docs.euler.finance](https://docs.euler.finance/concepts/financial/interest-rates?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

Risks and what to monitor

  • Oracle & liquidation efficacy (per vault): mispriced oracles or thin auctions can create bad debt; check the chosen oracle and penalty mech. [oai_citation:9‡docs.euler.finance](https://docs.euler.finance/concepts/core/vaults/?utm_source=chatgpt.com)
  • Utilisation & exit liquidity: high U can delay withdrawals; track vault-level U and IRM steepness. [oai_citation:10‡docs.euler.finance](https://docs.euler.finance/concepts/financial/interest-rates?utm_source=chatgpt.com)
  • Collateral map changes: liability vaults can update accepted collateral; watch governance and audit trails (plus bug bounty scope). [oai_citation:11‡Cantina](https://cantina.xyz/bounties/4d285eee-602e-440a-845e-25e155cec26a?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

Closing — why Euler matters (with current stats)

As of 31 Oct 2025, Euler V2 is securing roughly $3.26 billion TVL with $1.63 billion borrowed, and is generating an annualized fee run-rate around $137 million according to DefiLlama’s protocol dashboard. These figures underscore not only scale but also sustained usage across chains and vaults. [oai_citation:12‡defillama.com](https://defillama.com/protocol/euler-v2) Under the hood, the combination of EVK (to spin up isolated, configurable vaults) and the EVC (to compose them safely with batching and account controls) makes Euler less a single app and more a credit infrastructure layer for DeFi. For treasuries, DAOs, and funds, that means tailored interest-rate models, asset-specific risk boxes, and the ability to construct bespoke borrowing paths—without inheriting pool-wide contagion.
